Data sharing in Africa:
Drawing lessons from the Covid-19 response

Morine Amutorine
Data Science Africa Visiting Fellowship

Introduction

The apparent urgency caused by the Covid19 pandemic has been a catalyst for creative and novel approaches to data access and use, with the aim of supporting decision-makers to act fast and decisively. Countries across Africa have, and are still developing, innovative uses of data and statistics to effective emergency response, improving surveillance, aiding decision making and more.

  • There is an opportunity to learn from the use of data science for Covid19 response to benefit both policymakers and data scientists across Africa, helping to foster more effective data sharing collaborations and to support data-enabled innovations.

About the research

Data Science Africa, having built a strong foundation of data literacy, created and nurtured a community of African data scientists, has started to engage and initiate programs aimed at strengthening the foundations of the digital economy across Africa through supporting research aimed at effective data policy. Under the DSA research visits program, researchers are seeking to discover, study and draw lessons and best practices from the data driven Covid-19 response solutions in Africa to serve as â€¦ for the data science community in Africa.

Objective

Identify and engage with different actors (enablers, solution providers, data producers and curators, facilitators/coordinators and decision makers) that have been involved in the various solutions, to help the team to;

  • To harmonize approaches, collaborations, skills and agreements that go into data driven innovation and solutioning.

  • To identify and reflect on the barriers encountered and brainstorm ways in which they can be addressed.

  • Draw lessons from the experiences that can support safe and effective data use.

  • Make recommendations to inform the future development of data policies for increased data sharing for social good in the African context.
